## Cost Categories (Horizon Europe)

| Category | Description | Typical % of Budget | Rules |
|----------|-------------|--------------------|----- |
| **A. Personnel** | Salaries, daily rates | 60-70% | Based on actual costs or unit costs (per country) |
| **B. Subcontracting** | Work outsourced to third parties | 0-10% | Must be justified; core tasks cannot be subcontracted |
| **C. Purchase costs** | Equipment, consumables, travel | 10-20% | Equipment: depreciation only; Travel: actual costs |
| **D. Other goods & services** | Licenses, access fees, dissemination | 5-10% | Must be justified |
| **E. Indirect costs** | Overheads | 25% flat rate | Automatic: 25% of (A+B+C+D minus subcontracting) |

## Budget Rules & Red Flags

- **No partner < 3% of total budget** → signals minimal involvement; evaluators will question
- **No partner > 40% of total budget** → signals concentration; poor distribution
- **Coordinator typically 15-25%** of total budget
- **SMEs**: Ensure adequate budget for their contribution; too little = token participation
- **Subcontracting > 20% of a partner's budget** → requires strong justification
- **Travel**: Budget ~EUR 2,000-3,000 per person per project meeting (EU travel)
- **Equipment**: Only depreciation during project lifetime; full purchase only if justified

## Budget Sanity Checks

- [ ] Total budget is within the call's indicative budget range
- [ ] Personnel costs are ≥60% of total
- [ ] Indirect costs are exactly 25% of eligible direct costs (minus subcontracting)
- [ ] No partner below 3% threshold
- [ ] No partner above 40% threshold
- [ ] Coordinator budget is proportional to management effort
- [ ] SME budgets reflect meaningful participation
- [ ] Travel budget accounts for ~[N] consortium meetings + relevant conferences
- [ ] Equipment is depreciation-based (not full purchase) unless justified
- [ ] Subcontracting is justified and does not cover core research tasks
