# Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) & Gender Dimension Checklist

> **Used by**: Compliance Reviewer, Excellence Architect, Impact Specialist
> **Source**: Horizon Europe regulation, EU Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852), Gender Equality Plan requirements

---

## Part A: Do No Significant Harm (DNSH) Principle

The DNSH principle requires that funded activities do not significantly harm any of the six EU environmental objectives. Even for Cluster 3 (security) proposals, DNSH compliance must be addressed.

### The Six Environmental Objectives

For each objective, assess whether the proposal's activities and expected results could cause significant harm:

| # | Environmental Objective | Potential Harm? | Justification |
|---|------------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| 1 | Climate change mitigation | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |
| 2 | Climate change adaptation | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |
| 3 | Sustainable use and protection of water and marine resources | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |
| 4 | Transition to a circular economy | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |
| 5 | Pollution prevention and control | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |
| 6 | Protection and restoration of biodiversity and ecosystems | [ ] Yes [ ] No | |

### DNSH Assessment Guide

For each "Yes" above, provide:
1. **Nature of potential harm**: What activity/output could cause harm?
2. **Mitigation measures**: What steps will the project take to avoid or minimize harm?
3. **Net assessment**: After mitigation, is harm still "significant"?

### Common DNSH Considerations for CL3 (Security) Proposals

- **Computing/AI workloads**: Energy consumption of large-scale AI training/inference → address with efficient computing, green hosting
- **Hardware prototypes**: E-waste from sensor/device prototypes → address with circular economy principles, recycling plans
- **Travel**: Carbon footprint of consortium meetings → address with hybrid meeting policy
- **Surveillance technologies**: Environmental monitoring side-effects → typically minimal, but address if applicable

### Minimum Text for Proposal

The proposal should include a dedicated paragraph (typically in Section 1.3 Methodology or 2.1 Impact) stating:

> "The project activities have been assessed against the 'Do No Significant Harm' principle as defined in the EU Taxonomy Regulation (2020/852). [Brief assessment]. The project will [mitigation measures]."

## Part B: Gender Dimension

### B.1 Gender Equality Plan (GEP) — Institutional Level

Since 2022, all applicant organizations (public bodies, HEIs, research organizations) must have a Gender Equality Plan. This is an **eligibility criterion**, not an evaluation criterion.

- [ ] All eligible partner organizations have confirmed they have a GEP in place
- [ ] GEP status is noted in Part B-2 (consortium description) for each partner

### B.2 Gender Dimension in Research Content — Evaluation Criterion

This is evaluated under **Criterion 1: Excellence** (sub-criterion 1.5). The proposal must:

- [ ] **Analyse whether sex/gender is relevant** to the research objectives, methodology, and expected outcomes
- [ ] **If relevant**: Describe how sex and/or gender analysis is integrated into the work plan
- [ ] **If not relevant**: Provide a brief, explicit justification for why sex/gender analysis does not apply

### B.3 Common Gender Dimensions in CL3 Proposals

| Research Area | Gender-Relevant Considerations |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Border security | Gender-differentiated threat assessment; gender-based violence at borders; bias in biometric systems across demographics |
| Counter-terrorism | Gendered radicalization pathways; gender differences in online extremism patterns |
| Cybercrime/cybersecurity | Gender gap in cybersecurity workforce; gendered patterns in cyber victimization |
| Disaster resilience | Gender-differentiated vulnerability and response; gendered access to early warning systems |
| AI/Machine learning | Gender bias in training data; fairness metrics across demographic groups |
| Surveillance/monitoring | Disproportionate impact on women and marginalized groups; privacy considerations |

### B.4 Gender Balance in the Consortium

While not a formal criterion, evaluators notice and appreciate:

- [ ] Gender-balanced project leadership (Coordinator, WP leaders)
- [ ] Gender balance in the advisory board (if applicable)
- [ ] Gender-disaggregated reporting planned for project KPIs

### Minimum Text for Proposal

Include in Section 1.3 or a dedicated sub-section:

> "**Gender dimension**: [Analysis of whether/how sex and gender are relevant to the research content]. The project will integrate gender analysis by [specific measures, e.g., gender-disaggregated data collection, bias testing across demographic groups, gender-balanced user testing panels]. The consortium's key personnel include [X women and Y men] in leadership positions."
