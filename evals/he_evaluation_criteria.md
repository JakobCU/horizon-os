# Horizon Europe — Evaluation Criteria Checklist

> **Used by**: Compliance Reviewer, Coordinator Hub (checkpoint enforcement)
> **Source**: Horizon Europe General Annexes, Section D — Evaluation Criteria for RIA/IA

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Excellent — proposal successfully addresses all relevant aspects of the criterion |
| 4 | Very Good — proposal addresses the criterion very well, but with minor shortcomings |
| 3 | Good — proposal addresses the criterion well, but with notable shortcomings |
| 2 | Fair — proposal broadly addresses the criterion, but with significant weaknesses |
| 1 | Poor — the criterion is inadequately addressed, or there are serious inherent weaknesses |
| 0 | Fail — proposal fails to address the criterion, cannot be assessed due to missing information |

**Threshold**: 4/5 per criterion, 12/15 overall (for most CL3 calls — verify per call)

---

## Criterion 1: Excellence (Weight: typically 50% for RIA)

### 1.1 Clarity and pertinence of objectives

- [ ] Objectives are clearly stated and SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- [ ] Objectives are directly linked to the call's expected outcomes
- [ ] Each objective maps to specific WPs and deliverables
- [ ] Objectives are ambitious yet realistic

### 1.2 Soundness of the proposed methodology

- [ ] The methodology is clearly described and appropriate
- [ ] Research methods are state-of-the-art and well-justified
- [ ] Interdisciplinary approach is evident where needed
- [ ] The methodology addresses uncertainty and risk
- [ ] TRL progression is realistic and well-planned

### 1.3 Quality of the state-of-the-art analysis

- [ ] Comprehensive review of relevant research and innovation
- [ ] Clear identification of gaps and limitations in current approaches
- [ ] Positioning of the proposal relative to past/ongoing EU projects (CORDIS)
- [ ] References to recent peer-reviewed publications

### 1.4 Novelty and ambition — beyond the state of the art

- [ ] Clear articulation of what is novel/innovative
- [ ] Explains how the project goes beyond existing solutions
- [ ] Innovation is at the right level (not incremental, not science fiction)

### 1.5 Gender dimension in research content

- [ ] Sex/gender analysis is integrated where relevant
- [ ] If not relevant, a clear justification is provided
- [ ] See also: `evals/dnsh_and_gender_checklist.md`

### 1.6 Open science practices

- [ ] Immediate open access to publications (Plan S compliant)
- [ ] Research data management plan outlined
- [ ] FAIR data principles addressed
- [ ] Justified exceptions for restricted/classified data
- [ ] See: `knowledge/definitions/open_science_practices.md`

### 1.7 Interdisciplinary approach

- [ ] Integration of different disciplines is clear
- [ ] The consortium composition supports interdisciplinarity
- [ ] SSH (Social Sciences & Humanities) integration where appropriate

## Criterion 2: Impact (Weight: typically 30% for RIA)

### 2.1 Credibility of pathways to expected outcomes and impacts

- [ ] Impact pathways are described using the PEDR framework (Pathway, Effect, Diffusion, Result)
- [ ] Logical chain from outputs → outcomes → impacts is clear
- [ ] Quantitative indicators/KPIs are defined where possible
- [ ] Timeline for impact realization is realistic
- [ ] See: `templates/pedr_framework.md`

### 2.2 Suitability and quality of dissemination and exploitation measures

- [ ] Target audiences are identified (scientific, policy, industry, public)
- [ ] Dissemination plan is concrete (channels, timing, responsibilities)
- [ ] Exploitation strategy per key result (commercialization, policy uptake, standardization)
- [ ] Each exploitable result has an identified owner/partner
- [ ] Business model or sustainability plan beyond the project lifetime

### 2.3 Communication strategy

- [ ] Separate from dissemination — focuses on general public awareness
- [ ] Concrete activities (website, social media, events, media engagement)
- [ ] Budget allocated for communication

### 2.4 Contribution to EU policy and strategy

- [ ] Clear link to EU Security Union Strategy / relevant EU policies
- [ ] Contribution to European standardization efforts
- [ ] Policy recommendations pathway

## Criterion 3: Quality and Efficiency of Implementation (Weight: typically 20% for RIA)

### 3.1 Quality and effectiveness of the work plan

- [ ] WP structure is logical and efficient
- [ ] WPs have clear objectives, tasks, deliverables, and milestones
- [ ] Dependencies between WPs are identified and managed
- [ ] GANTT chart is clear and realistic
- [ ] Deliverable schedule is balanced (no all-at-the-end clustering)

### 3.2 Appropriateness of management structures and procedures

- [ ] Governance structure is clear (GA, SC, WP leaders, advisory boards)
- [ ] Decision-making and conflict resolution procedures defined
- [ ] Risk management plan with identified risks and mitigations
- [ ] Quality assurance procedures
- [ ] Innovation management strategy

### 3.3 Complementarity of participants and consortium as a whole

- [ ] Each partner has a clearly defined and non-redundant role
- [ ] The consortium covers the full value chain (research → validation → deployment)
- [ ] Geographic and organizational diversity
- [ ] End-user involvement is credible and meaningful
- [ ] Partner track record supports their assigned tasks

### 3.4 Appropriateness of the allocated resources

- [ ] Budget is proportional to tasks and justified
- [ ] Person-months are realistic for the work described
- [ ] Major cost items (equipment, subcontracting) are justified
- [ ] Budget distribution across partners is balanced and fair
- [ ] No partner has <3% or >40% of total budget (red flag)
