# CORTEX Infographic — DALL-E Prompt

## Prompt

A clean, professional infographic poster for a European research project called "CORTEX — Cognitive Resilient Teaming for Extreme Search and Rescue." The visual should work as both an internal pitch visual and a consortium overview.

**Layout — top to bottom, three zones:**

**Top zone — the problem:** A stylized disaster scene (collapsed building, dust, partial darkness) with a firefighter in full PPE operating a tablet/controller alongside a search-and-rescue robot (small UGV) and a drone overhead. The firefighter looks fatigued and overwhelmed — multiple data streams and alerts are flooding a small screen. Visual cue: red/orange warning tones, cognitive overload represented by swirling abstract data fragments around the operator's head.

**Middle zone — the CORTEX solution (the core of the infographic):** A clean architectural diagram in flat design style showing the closed-loop system. Five connected modules arranged in a horizontal flow, each as a distinct rounded-rectangle icon with a simple pictogram inside:
1. "Cognitive Twin" — a biosensor wearable icon with a heartbeat wave morphing into a brain-wave pattern, color: teal
2. "Adaptive Autonomy Manager" — a dial/slider icon showing a spectrum from "human control" to "robot autonomy," color: blue
3. "Adaptive C2 Overlay" — a command dashboard icon with highlighted priority elements, color: indigo
4. "XR Training" — a VR headset icon with a human and robot silhouette training together, color: violet
5. "Mission Replay" — a timeline/playback icon with synchronized data layers, color: purple

Arrows connect the modules in a loop, with "Responder State" as the signal flowing from the Cognitive Twin through the whole system and back. A central label reads "The Missing Human-AI Teaming Layer." The overall feel is that human cognitive state is the control signal driving the entire architecture.

**Bottom zone — consortium and impact:** A simplified map of Europe with 8-9 dots marking partner countries (Austria, Spain, Italy, Portugal, Belgium, Germany, Sweden, Netherlands). Below the map: "9 Partners | 8 Countries | 36 Months | EUR 4M | TRL 7-8". Small icons representing: 4 end-user organizations (fire helmet, ambulance), 3 technology SMEs (gear/chip), 1 university (book), 1 coordinator (star). The Horizon Europe logo and EU flag are placed in the bottom corner.

**Style:** Flat design, modern data-visualization aesthetic. Color palette: dark navy background with teal, blue, indigo, violet, and white accents. No photorealism — clean vector-style illustration. Typography is sans-serif, bold headers. The overall tone is serious, technological, and human-centered — not cold or purely robotic. The human responder is always central and prominent, never subordinate to the technology.

**Text on the infographic (minimal):**
- Title: "CORTEX"
- Subtitle: "Cognitive Resilient Teaming for Extreme Search and Rescue"
- Module labels: "Cognitive Twin", "Adaptive Autonomy", "Adaptive C2", "XR Training", "Mission Replay"
- Central tagline: "Closing the loop between the responder, the robot, and the command post"
- Bottom: "Horizon Europe | HORIZON-CL3-2026-01-DRS-03"

**Aspect ratio:** 2:3 (portrait poster format)

---

## Notes for generation

- DALL-E may struggle with precise text rendering — expect to fix text labels manually in post-production (Figma, Canva, PowerPoint)
- If text comes out garbled, regenerate with the prompt modified to say "no text, leave blank label areas for text to be added later"
- Consider generating the three zones separately and compositing them for better quality
- For a cleaner result, you could also generate just the middle zone (architecture diagram) and build the rest in a design tool

## Alternative: Text-free version prompt

Same as above but replace all text instructions with: "Leave clean blank spaces where labels would go — no text, no letters, no words anywhere in the image. The layout should have clear placeholder areas for titles, labels, and captions to be added in post-production."
