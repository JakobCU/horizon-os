## Candidate 6.3: Eticas Research and Consulting (Spain)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Eticas Research and Consulting S.L. (trading as Eticas AI) |
| **Country** | Spain (Barcelona; also offices in Amsterdam and Belgrade) |
| **Type** | SME / Specialized Consultancy (~30 staff) |
| **Website** | https://eticas.ai / https://www.eticasconsulting.com |
| **Key Person** | Dr. Gemma Galdon Clavell (Founder & CEO) |

**Why they fit:**

- **Purpose-built for this role.** Eticas was founded in 2012 as a university spin-off (Universitat de Barcelona, Sociology Department) specifically to analyze the legal, ethical, and social impact of security policy, innovation, and technology development. Security/safety-domain ELSI is their core business, not a sideline.
- **Pioneered the ethics work package model for EU security projects.** Eticas developed the first Ethics Work Package as part of an FP7 Security project in 2011. They have since served as the ethics partner in 10+ EC-funded projects across FP7, H2020, and Horizon Europe.
- **Directly relevant H2020 project track record:**
  - **SATIE** (H2020, 832969) -- Security of Air Transport Infrastructure of Europe. Eticas led ethics requirements and data protection compliance for security technology development and validation.
  - **IMPETUS** (H2020, 883286) -- Intelligent Management of Processes, Ethics and Technology for Urban Safety. Eticas contributed to the ethics dimension of smart city security capabilities, balancing data collection needs with privacy. The project included police, first responders, and municipalities across 17 partners / 11 countries.
  - **CounteR** (H2020) -- Counter-terrorism/radicalisation project. Eticas contributed responsible research and innovation assessment.
  - **ACHILLES** (Horizon Europe) -- Eticas leads ethical and legal compliance, developing frameworks for AI systems' adherence to GDPR and the EU AI Act.
- **Algorithmic auditing and AI Act expertise.** Eticas has developed proprietary methodologies for algorithmic impact assessment and AI auditing, directly applicable to CORTEX's adaptive autonomy system (which will be classified as high-risk under the EU AI Act).
- **First responder technology experience.** Eticas has won three H2020 projects specifically related to AI use and technology improvement for first responders in emergencies.
- **SME status.** Adds to the consortium's SME count -- a scored criterion.

**Risk flags:**
- **Dual entity structure.** Eticas operates under multiple branding (Eticas Research and Consulting S.L., Eticas AI, eticasconsulting.com, eticas.ai). Need to clarify which legal entity would sign the Grant Agreement and confirm its SME status.
- **Certificate issue noted.** During research, their eticasconsulting.com SSL certificate had expired. Minor but may indicate stretched internal IT resources.
- **Spain already represented.** If Robotnik (Spain) is selected for GAP-01, having Eticas adds a second Spanish partner. Not a problem per se (different domains, different cities -- Valencia vs Barcelona), but reduces geographic novelty.
- **Consultancy depth.** At ~30 staff, a EUR 250k commitment is substantial. Need to confirm dedicated senior personnel availability for a 36-month WP lead role.

**Recommendation: STRONG**

Eticas is one of the most experienced ELSI consultancies in the European security research ecosystem. Their combination of security-domain specialization, H2020 project pedigree in first responder technology, algorithmic auditing methodology, and AI Act expertise makes them an excellent WP6 lead candidate. The fact that they pioneered the "Ethics Work Package" concept in FP7 Security is a strong differentiator.

## Candidate 6.4: University of Twente -- Philosophy of Technology Group (Netherlands)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Universiteit Twente, Faculty of Behavioural, Management and Social Sciences (BMS), Philosophy Section -- Ethics and Technology Track |
| **Country** | Netherlands (Enschede) |
| **Type** | University |
| **Website** | https://www.utwente.nl/en/bms/phil/ |
| **Key Person** | Prof. Philip Brey (Chair of Philosophy of Technology) |

**Why they fit:**

- **Coordinated SIENNA -- the flagship EU ethics-of-technology project.** Prof. Philip Brey coordinated the SIENNA project (H2020, 741716, EUR ~4M, 11 core partners + 2 associates), which addressed ethical and human rights challenges posed by AI, robotics, and human enhancement technologies. SIENNA produced the "Ethics by Design" methodology that the European Commission adopted into its Horizon Europe ethics review procedure. This is one of the highest-impact EU ethics projects ever funded.
- **Partner in TechEthos (H2020, 101006249) -- coordinated by AIT.** This is a critical connection: AIT coordinated the TechEthos project (EUR ~4M), and the University of Twente was a consortium partner (EUR ~297k allocation). TechEthos addressed ethics of emerging technologies with high socio-economic impact. **This means AIT and UT have a proven working relationship**, which dramatically reduces consortium integration risk.
- **UT led WP5 in TechEthos** on the Societal Readiness Tool, demonstrating WP leadership capability within an AIT-coordinated project.
- **Ethics and Epistemology of AI Initiative.** UT runs a dedicated interdisciplinary research initiative on AI ethics, covering value-sensitive design, trustworthy AI, and responsible innovation.
- **SHERPA project connection.** UT's SIENNA project collaborated closely with the SHERPA project (Shaping Ethical Dimensions of Smart Information Systems), demonstrating network depth in the EU ethics community.
- **Dual competence: philosophy + engineering.** UT is a technical university. The Philosophy of Technology group sits within a faculty that also covers human factors, behavioural science, and social sciences of technology. This interdisciplinarity means they can address not just abstract ethical questions but also practical human-technology interaction issues relevant to CORTEX's biosignal monitoring and adaptive autonomy.
- **Dutch partner.** Adds Netherlands to the consortium -- good geographic diversity.

**Risk flags:**
- **Academic orientation.** UT's strength is in ethical frameworks, guidelines, and societal readiness assessment -- academic deliverables. They may be less experienced with the operational DPIA, AI Act compliance checklist, and GDPR legal analysis that WP6 requires. A complementary legal partner (or AIT's own IP & Legal expertise) may be needed for strictly legal deliverables.
- **Not a security-domain specialist.** SIENNA and TechEthos were broad "ethics of technology" projects, not Cluster 3 security projects. UT has not led an ethics WP in a first-responder or disaster-response project specifically.
- **Philip Brey dependency.** Much of UT's profile in this space depends on Prof. Brey's personal reputation and involvement. Need to confirm he would be named PI and allocate sufficient PMs.
- **Budget fit.** UT received EUR ~297k in TechEthos. A EUR 250k allocation for CORTEX WP6 lead is consistent and realistic.

**Recommendation: STRONG**

The existing AIT-UT working relationship from TechEthos is a major asset -- evaluators value proven consortium partnerships. UT's SIENNA coordination demonstrates they can lead large ethics activities. The risk is that their expertise is more philosophical/societal than legal/regulatory; pairing them with a legal specialist for GDPR/AI Act compliance tasks within WP6 would create the strongest configuration. The Netherlands adds valuable geographic diversity.

## Candidate 6.5: PRIVANOVA SAS (France)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | PRIVANOVA SAS |
| **Country** | France (Paris) |
| **Type** | Specialized Consultancy (boutique SME) |
| **Website** | https://www.privanova.com |
| **Key Persons** | Djordje Djokic (Co-founder, Senior Data Protection Executive); Anastasiya Kiseleva (Senior Ethics, Regulatory & Policy Lead) |

**Why they fit:**

- **25+ FP7/H2020/HE consortia.** PRIVANOVA's team has initiated, managed, or participated in more than 25 EU project consortia. This is one of the deepest EU project track records of any ethics-focused consultancy.
- **Former INTERPOL and UN professionals.** The team includes senior staff with law enforcement and international organization backgrounds, giving them operational understanding of security contexts that purely academic partners lack.
- **Specific security project roles:**
  - **CC-DRIVER** (H2020) -- Cybercriminality project. PRIVANOVA participated in WP6 on Ethics, Data Protection and Socio-Economic Impact Assessment. Led the establishment and management of the Ethics Advisory Board (EAB) and managed quarterly EAB meetings.
  - **MARVEL** (H2020, 957337) -- Smart cities multimodal analytics. PRIVANOVA implemented Privacy-by-Design approach, monitored data protection compliance, and managed the Ethics Advisory Board.
  - **IAMI** (Horizon Europe, 101168272) -- Identity attributes for law enforcement. PRIVANOVA leads regulatory compliance (EU AI Act for AI components), conducts risk assessments, and develops exploitation strategies.
  - **SafeHorizon** (Horizon Europe) -- Cybercrime prevention (EUR ~4M, 2024-2027). PRIVANOVA leads Dissemination, Communication & Exploitation and coordinates the LEA Projects Cluster.
  - **CYRENE, AI4HealthSec, DIGICARE4YOU** -- Additional projects where PRIVANOVA serves as legal and ethics manager.
- **DPIA specialist.** PRIVANOVA explicitly offers DPIA implementation services -- both project-wide DPIAs and partner-specific assessments. This is directly needed for CORTEX's biosignal (health data) processing.
- **DPO services.** Can serve as the project's Data Protection Officer, managing GDPR compliance across the consortium.
- **EU AI Act expertise.** The IAMI project specifically tasks PRIVANOVA with ensuring AI components comply with the EU AI Act -- directly transferable to CORTEX's adaptive autonomy and cognitive twin systems.
- **LEA Projects Cluster leadership.** PRIVANOVA leads a cross-project cluster connecting EU-funded security projects, demonstrating convening power and network access.

**Risk flags:**
- **Boutique size.** PRIVANOVA is a small consultancy. While their track record is impressive, a EUR 250k WP lead commitment over 36 months requires dedicated senior personnel. Need to verify that they are not overcommitted across their 25+ project portfolio.
- **Law enforcement orientation.** Many of PRIVANOVA's projects are law-enforcement/cybersecurity focused (CC-DRIVER, IAMI, SafeHorizon). CORTEX is civil protection/disaster response, not law enforcement. The ethics issues overlap (AI, biometrics, data protection) but the operational context differs. Need to verify that PRIVANOVA can credibly address first-responder workplace monitoring ethics, not just LEA surveillance ethics.
- **French partner.** France is well-represented in CL3 consortia. Not a problem, but does not add geographic novelty.
- **Academic credibility.** As a consultancy rather than a university, PRIVANOVA may face evaluator skepticism about the "depth" of their ethics analysis compared to an academic partner. Mitigated by their operational experience.

**Recommendation: GOOD**

PRIVANOVA brings unmatched operational experience in EU security project ethics management -- DPIA delivery, EAB management, AI Act compliance, Privacy-by-Design. Their law enforcement/INTERPOL background gives them credibility with security-domain evaluators. The risk is that they are more of an "ethics compliance manager" than an "ethics researcher" -- excellent at DPIAs and EABs but potentially less strong on publishing novel ethical frameworks. For CORTEX, where the WP6 deliverables are practical (DPIA, AI Act checklist, data policy, ethical governance framework), this operational orientation is arguably an advantage.

## Candidate 6.6: KU Leuven -- Centre for IT & IP Law (CiTiP) (Belgium)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | KU Leuven, Faculty of Law and Criminology, Centre for IT & IP Law (CiTiP) |
| **Country** | Belgium (Leuven) |
| **Type** | University (research centre within KU Leuven) |
| **Website** | https://www.law.kuleuven.be/citip/en |
| **Key Persons** | Prof. Peggy Valcke; Prof. Anton Vedder; 40+ researchers |

**Why they fit:**

- **40+ researchers specializing in IT law, data protection, and AI governance.** CiTiP is one of the largest dedicated IT law research centres in Europe, with critical mass that smaller groups cannot match.
- **Direct security-domain H2020/HE project track record:**
  - **STARLIGHT** (H2020) -- Building AI capability for law enforcement agencies. CiTiP ensures legal, ethical, and societal values are upheld. PI: Prof. Peggy Valcke.
  - **ALIGNER** (H2020, 101020574) -- AI roadmap for policing and law enforcement. CiTiP identifies ethical, societal, and legal implications of AI use in law enforcement. PI: Prof. Peggy Valcke.
  - **PRAETORIAN** (H2020, 101021274) -- Critical infrastructure protection against physical and cyber threats. CiTiP addresses multidimensional security dimensions. PI: Prof. Anton Vedder.
  - **DANTE** (H2020) -- Detection of financial crime. KU Leuven CiTiP is a consortium partner providing legal and ethical expertise.
  - **CyberSANE** -- Cybersecurity project with CiTiP involvement.
  - **GLOMICAVE** -- ELSI expertise for multi-omics/AI.
- **AI Act and GDPR specialist.** CiTiP's research pillars include "Artificial Intelligence & Autonomous Systems" and "Data Protection & Privacy" -- the exact legal domains CORTEX's WP6 requires.
- **Internationally renowned.** CiTiP is explicitly described as "internationally renowned for its expertise in Artificial Intelligence & Autonomous systems, Data Protection & Privacy, and (Cyber)security."
- **KU Leuven institutional weight.** KU Leuven is the #1 Horizon Europe grant winner among European universities. CiTiP benefits from world-class institutional support for EU project administration.
- **Belgian partner.** Belgium adds a Benelux dimension; evaluators may note the proximity to EU institutions as an advantage for policy impact.

**Risk flags:**
- **Belgium already represented.** VUB-LSTS (Round 1 candidate) is also Belgian. Having both KU Leuven CiTiP and VUB-LSTS would over-represent Belgium. Choose one.
- **Law enforcement focus.** Like PRIVANOVA, CiTiP's security projects (STARLIGHT, ALIGNER, DANTE) are law enforcement-oriented, not civil protection/disaster response. The ethics of biosignal monitoring for firefighter safety is a different context from police AI surveillance. CiTiP would need to demonstrate adaptability.
- **Large centre overhead.** With 40+ researchers, CiTiP may assign junior researchers to a EUR 250k project. Need to confirm PI commitment and seniority of assigned staff.
- **Possible overcommitment.** With multiple ongoing H2020/HE projects, CiTiP may have limited bandwidth for WP6 leadership. Need to verify capacity.

**Recommendation: GOOD**

CiTiP is a heavyweight in IT law and AI governance with an impressive CL3 security project portfolio. The main concern is geographic overlap with VUB-LSTS (both Belgian) and the law enforcement orientation of their existing projects. If VUB-LSTS is not selected from Round 1, CiTiP is a strong alternative from the same country with arguably deeper security-domain experience. If VUB-LSTS is already selected, CiTiP should not be added.

## Candidate 6.7: Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna -- DIRPOLIS Institute, Ethics of Technology Section (Italy)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna di Studi Universitari e di Perfezionamento, DIRPOLIS Institute (Institute of Law, Politics and Development), Ethics of Technology Section |
| **Country** | Italy (Pisa) |
| **Type** | University (elite graduate school, public institution) |
| **Website** | https://www.santannapisa.it/en/institute/dirpolis/ethics-tecnology |
| **Key Person** | Prof. Alberto Pirni (roboethics, technology ethics) |

**Why they fit:**

- **Roboethics pioneer in Europe.** The Ethics of Technology section at DIRPOLIS specifically researches the ethics of "robotic systems and technologies (biomedical applications, bioinformatics, biorobotics, biomechatronics)." This is directly relevant to CORTEX's adaptive autonomy manager and human-robot teaming.
- **RoboLaw project (FP7, 289092).** Sant'Anna coordinated the landmark RoboLaw project -- "Regulating Emerging Robotic Technologies in Europe: Robotics facing Law and Ethics." This produced the "Guidelines on Regulating Robotics" used by European institutions. RoboLaw is the foundational EU project on robot regulation.
- **LEADs project (H2020-MSCA-ITN-2020).** Sant'Anna coordinates LEADs (Legality Attentive Data Scientists), training experts in AI, big data, ethical data mining, and legal practice. This demonstrates current H2020 coordination capability.
- **Cross-disciplinary strength.** Sant'Anna uniquely combines DIRPOLIS (law, politics, ethics) with TeCIP (engineering, robotics, perception) and the BioRobotics Institute under one roof. This means the ethics team has daily access to robotics engineers -- a rare advantage for grounding ethical analysis in technical reality.
- **Italian partner.** If Smartex (Pisa) is selected for GAP-03, Sant'Anna is literally in the same city, enabling local collaboration. Italy adds geographic diversity.
- **Value-sensitive design methodology.** The group explicitly works on "Design for Values" and responsible innovation methodologies, applicable to CORTEX's human-centered design approach.

**Risk flags:**
- **No CL3 security project track record.** RoboLaw was FP7 (2012-2014) and was about general robot regulation, not security/safety-critical systems specifically. LEADs is an MSCA training network, not a security research project. Sant'Anna's DIRPOLIS has not been a partner in a Cluster 3 / DRS project.
- **Small section.** The Ethics of Technology section within DIRPOLIS appears to be a small research group (4-5 researchers identified). Capacity for a EUR 250k WP lead role over 36 months may be stretched.
- **Primarily philosophical.** The group's strength is philosophical ethics and regulatory analysis, not operational compliance (DPIAs, AI Act conformity assessment). Would need to be complemented by a legal/compliance partner for practical deliverables.
- **RoboLaw age.** The flagship project is from 2012-2014 (FP7). More recent large-scale EU project leadership in the ethics/law domain is limited.
- **Italy potentially over-represented.** If Smartex (Pisa) and IES Solutions (Catania) are both selected, adding Sant'Anna (Pisa) creates a third Italian partner. Evaluators may flag national concentration.

**Recommendation: CONDITIONAL**

Sant'Anna's roboethics pedigree (RoboLaw) and the unique co-location of ethics and robotics research groups make them conceptually attractive for CORTEX. However, the lack of CL3 security project experience, the small team size, and the age of their flagship project weaken their candidacy compared to Eticas, UT, or PRIVANOVA. Best considered if: (a) the consortium specifically wants an Italian ethics partner co-located with Smartex, or (b) the roboethics angle is deemed more important than security-domain ELSI track record.

## Strategic Recommendations

### Option A: Maximum Evaluator Impact (recommended)
**Select: University of Twente**
- Rationale: The existing AIT-UT working relationship from TechEthos is gold for evaluators -- it demonstrates a "proven team." UT's SIENNA coordination and Ethics by Design methodology (adopted by the EC) carry enormous institutional credibility. The Netherlands adds geographic diversity.
- Complement: Have AIT's own IP & Legal expert handle the strictly legal deliverables (DPIA, AI Act checklist) within WP6, or allocate a small WP6 task to a legal subcontractor.

### Option B: Maximum Operational Deliverable Quality
**Select: Eticas Research**
- Rationale: If WP6 is expected to produce practical, operational deliverables (DPIA documents, AI Act conformity checklists, ethics monitoring reports) rather than academic ethics frameworks, Eticas is the strongest choice. They have done this exact work in 10+ security projects. Their algorithmic auditing methodology is directly applicable to CORTEX's AI components.
- Note: Also adds to SME count.

### Option C: Hedge -- Split WP6
**Select: University of Twente (WP6 lead, ethical frameworks + societal readiness) + PRIVANOVA (WP6 task lead, DPIA + data protection compliance)**
- Rationale: Combines academic depth with operational compliance. UT handles ethical governance framework, societal readiness assessment, and Ethics by Design methodology. PRIVANOVA handles DPIA, DPO, EAB management, and AI Act compliance documentation.
- Risk: Two partners for EUR 250k is tight. Would need EUR ~150k for UT + EUR ~100k for PRIVANOVA, or increase overall WP6 budget to EUR 300-350k.

### If Round 1 candidates remain preferred:
- **Trilateral Research** remains the single strongest all-round candidate if the IE entity is confirmed as eligible.
- **VUB-LSTS** remains excellent but creates Belgium concentration if other Belgian partners are considered.
