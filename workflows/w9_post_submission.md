# W9: Post-Submission & Evaluation Response

> **Owner**: Coordinator Hub
> **Trigger**: User confirms proposal submitted via the Funding & Tenders Portal
> **Purpose**: Prepare for evaluation outcome, rebuttal (if invited), and lessons learned

---

## Why This Exists

Submission is not the end. Between submission and grant agreement, there are critical steps most teams neglect — and if the proposal is rejected, the work done here determines whether the next attempt succeeds or repeats the same mistakes.

## Workflow Steps

### Phase A: Immediate Post-Submission (Day 1-7)

#### Step 1: Archive & Document
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub
- **Action**:
  - [ ] Archive final submitted documents (Part B-1, B-2, budget, abstract)
  - [ ] Record submission timestamp and confirmation number
  - [ ] Update `output/grant_pipeline_tracker.md` with status `SUBMITTED`
  - [ ] Save final `partner_matrix.md` snapshot
  - [ ] Document any known weaknesses or compromises made during final assembly

#### Step 2: Thank Consortium
- **Actor**: User (Coordinator drafts)
- **Action**: Draft a "thank you" email to all partners confirming submission, next steps, and expected evaluation timeline

### Phase B: Evaluation Preparation (Submission → Results)

#### Step 3: Rebuttal Preparation (if applicable)
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub
- **Note**: Some CL3 calls invite applicants to respond to evaluator comments before final ranking. This is rare but high-impact when it happens.
- **Action**: Pre-identify likely weaknesses by re-reading the Compliance Reviewer's W7 report:
  - What issues did we fix but might still be visible?
  - What did we deliberately accept as risks?
  - What would WE critique if we were evaluators?
  - Prepare 1-paragraph rebuttals for top 5 anticipated criticisms

#### Step 4: Plan B Assessment
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub
- **Action**: If the proposal is not funded:
  - Which elements can be reused for the next call?
  - Should the consortium stay together or reconfigure?
  - Are there alternative funding instruments (national, bilateral, EDF, ISF)?
  - Is the next CL3 Work Programme likely to have a similar topic?

### Phase C: Evaluation Results Received

#### Step 5: Outcome Processing
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub
- **Decision tree**:

  | Outcome | Action |
  |---------|--------|
  | **Funded (above threshold, within budget)** | Proceed to grant agreement preparation |
  | **Reserve list** | Maintain consortium readiness; monitor for budget redistribution |
  | **Rejected (above threshold, no budget)** | Strong resubmission candidate — analyze evaluator comments for minor fixes |
  | **Rejected (below threshold)** | Deep analysis needed — which criterion scored lowest? |

#### Step 6: Evaluator Feedback Analysis
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub → Compliance Reviewer
- **Action**: Parse the Evaluation Summary Report (ESR):
  - Score per criterion
  - Evaluator comments per section
  - Specific weaknesses cited
  - Map each evaluator comment to our self-assessment from W7
  - Identify "surprises" — weaknesses we didn't anticipate

#### Step 7: Lessons Learned
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub
- **Action**: Document lessons for future proposals:
  - What scored well? (preserve these approaches)
  - What scored poorly? (fix these patterns)
  - Were there structural issues? (consortium, budget distribution, WP design)
  - Was the storyline clear to evaluators? (check against Narrative Strategist's arc)
  - Update agent prompts or eval checklists if a systemic issue is identified

### Phase D: Grant Agreement Preparation (if funded)

#### Step 8: Consortium Agreement
- **Actor**: IP & Legal Expert
- **Action**: Draft / coordinate Consortium Agreement covering:
  - IP ownership and access rights
  - Decision-making procedures
  - Financial arrangements
  - Confidentiality and publication procedures
  - Partner entry/exit provisions

#### Step 9: Grant Agreement Preparation (GAP)
- **Actor**: Coordinator Hub + User
- **Action**:
  - Respond to any EC requests for clarification
  - Finalize legal and financial information in the portal
  - Confirm partner PIC numbers and legal status
  - Finalize detailed budget breakdown
  - Address any ethics or security clearance requirements

## Exit Criteria
- [ ] Submission archived and documented
- [ ] Pipeline tracker updated
- [ ] Rebuttal preparation complete (if applicable)
- [ ] Plan B assessed
- [ ] (When results arrive) Evaluator feedback analyzed and lessons documented
- [ ] (If funded) Grant agreement preparation initiated
