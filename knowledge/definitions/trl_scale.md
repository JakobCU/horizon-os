# Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Scale

> **Source**: European Commission, Horizon Europe Work Programme General Annexes

| TRL | Definition | Description |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| TRL 1 | Basic principles observed | Scientific research begins to be translated into applied R&D |
| TRL 2 | Technology concept formulated | Practical applications identified, but speculative; no proof or detailed analysis |
| TRL 3 | Experimental proof of concept | Active R&D initiated; analytical and laboratory studies to validate predictions |
| TRL 4 | Technology validated in lab | Basic technological components integrated; tested in laboratory environment |
| TRL 5 | Technology validated in relevant environment | Basic technology components integrated with reasonably realistic supporting elements; tested in simulated or real environment |
| TRL 6 | Technology demonstrated in relevant environment | Representative model/prototype tested in relevant environment |
| TRL 7 | System prototype demonstration in operational environment | Prototype near or at planned operational system; tested in operational environment |
| TRL 8 | System complete and qualified | Technology proven to work in final form under expected conditions |
| TRL 9 | Actual system proven in operational environment | Technology applied in final form; proven through successful operations |

## Typical TRL Ranges by Instrument

| Instrument | Start TRL | End TRL | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-------|
| RIA (Research & Innovation Action) | 2-4 | 5-7 | Focus on research and validation |
| IA (Innovation Action) | 5-6 | 7-8 | Focus on demonstration and deployment |
| CSA (Coordination & Support Action) | N/A | N/A | No technology development |

## Usage in Proposals

- State the starting TRL for each key technology/component
- State the target TRL at project end
- Justify the TRL assessment with evidence (publications, prototypes, demonstrations)
- The TRL progression must be realistic for the project duration
