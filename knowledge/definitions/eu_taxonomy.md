# EU Taxonomy Regulation — Quick Reference

> **Source**: Regulation (EU) 2020/852
> **Relevance**: DNSH principle compliance in Horizon Europe proposals

## What is the EU Taxonomy?

A classification system establishing a list of environmentally sustainable economic activities. It defines six environmental objectives and requires that activities:
1. **Substantially contribute** to at least one objective
2. **Do No Significant Harm (DNSH)** to any of the other five
3. Comply with minimum social safeguards

## The Six Environmental Objectives

1. **Climate change mitigation** — reducing GHG emissions
2. **Climate change adaptation** — reducing vulnerability to climate change impacts
3. **Sustainable use and protection of water and marine resources**
4. **Transition to a circular economy** — waste prevention, reuse, recycling
5. **Pollution prevention and control** — beyond regulatory compliance
6. **Protection and restoration of biodiversity and ecosystems**

## Relevance to CL3 Security Proposals

Most CL3 proposals have LOW direct environmental impact, but the DNSH assessment is still required. Common considerations:
- Energy consumption of computing infrastructure (AI/ML workloads)
- E-waste from prototype hardware
- Travel emissions from consortium meetings
- Environmental impact of pilot/demonstration activities
