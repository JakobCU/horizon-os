# European Green Deal — Quick Reference

> **Source**: COM(2019) 640 final
> **Relevance**: DNSH compliance, cross-cutting requirement for all Horizon Europe proposals

## Key Points

- EU's growth strategy to become climate-neutral by 2050
- Covers all sectors: energy, industry, buildings, transport, food, ecosystems, biodiversity
- Underpins the EU Taxonomy and DNSH principle
- All Horizon Europe proposals must demonstrate alignment (or at minimum, no harm)

## Relevance to CL3 Security Proposals

- Direct relevance is typically low for security research
- However, proposals should demonstrate awareness and address:
  - Energy efficiency of computing/AI workloads
  - Sustainable procurement of hardware
  - Green meeting policies (hybrid/virtual)
  - Environmental co-benefits where applicable (e.g., environmental monitoring as a security-resilience dual-use)
- The DNSH assessment (`evals/dnsh_and_gender_checklist.md`) operationalizes Green Deal compliance
