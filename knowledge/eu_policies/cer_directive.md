# CER Directive — Critical Entities Resilience

> **Source**: Directive (EU) 2022/2557
> **Relevance**: CL3 proposals on critical infrastructure protection, disaster resilience

## Key Points

- Replaces the 2008 European Critical Infrastructure (ECI) Directive
- Covers **11 sectors**: energy, transport, banking, financial market infrastructure, health, drinking water, wastewater, digital infrastructure, public administration, space, food
- Member States must adopt national strategies for critical entities resilience
- Critical entities must carry out risk assessments and take resilience measures
- Establishes a framework for cross-border cooperation

## Relevance to CL3 Proposals

- Proposals addressing critical infrastructure protection should reference the CER Directive
- Technologies supporting risk assessment, incident response, or cross-sector resilience align directly
- Multi-hazard, all-hazards approach is preferred (not just cyber or physical separately)
- Link to NIS2 Directive for cyber aspects of critical entities

## Key Terminology

- **Critical entity**: An entity providing an essential service identified by a Member State
- **Resilience**: The ability to prevent, protect against, respond to, resist, mitigate, absorb, accommodate, and recover from an incident
- **Essential service**: A service crucial for the maintenance of vital societal functions or economic activities
